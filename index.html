Hello and namaste.

I am Sai Gattupalli and I am a fourth year PhD student in the Learning Sciences program at the College of Education at the University of Massachusetts Amherst. I am located at the Advanced Learning Technologies Lab↗ (N137 Furcolo) in Amherst, MA and work with Ivon Arroyo, EdD↗, Francisco Castro, PhD↗, Beverly Woolf, PhD↗, Danielle Allessio, PhD↗, Torrey Trust, PhD↗, Betsy McEneaney, PhD↗, Sharon Edwards, PhD↗ and Bob Maloy, EdD↗.

I develop Wearable Learning ↗, which is an online multiplayer embodied games platform for math learning that leverages on learning outcomes on both game play and game design practices. Using our educational platform, K12 students develop computational thinking, which is a crucial and much needed skill in the 21st century (Wing, 2006). Research participants, mostly children aka learners aka people from different cultures, economic backgrounds, identities, and from under-served populations of our societies, from - Boston and Holyoke, USA, Huzurabad, India, Cardoba, Argentina and Manila, the Philippines - participated in our game play and game design activities. Research has shown that higher learning outcomes can be achieved when educators use Wearable Learning. My research focuses on learner culture which when understood in nuanced contexts could lead to better learning experiences.

Current Research Questions

  • How does learner culture manifest in game design artifacts, and what do they indicate?

  • How do we define value attributions learners assign in artifacts?

  • How can researchers utilize findings to improve learning experience across cultures?

It is my motto to make learning more equitable and accessible, and effective in culturally-relevant ways. For research collaborations and research related opportunities in Learning Sciences STEM foci areas such as game based learning, learner culture, computational thinking, and advanced learning technologies, or for any other inquiries, email me at sgattupalli at umass dot eduor go here.
